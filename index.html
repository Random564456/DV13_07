Criminal offences in Australia are often penalised through monetary fines. Each state and territory independently administers fines as part of their legal and enforcement system. This interactive map allows you to explore:

This chart illustrates the distribution of criminal offence fines issued across each month of the year. Each data point represents the total number of fines delivered within that month, providing insights into seasonal or administrative trends in law enforcement.

Automated camera systems, particularly mobile and fixed cameras, are responsible for the majority of traffic fines in Australia, accounting for over 3 million violations combined.

In contrast, traditional police-issued fines and other systems like red light or speed cameras contribute significantly less, highlighting a strong reliance on automated enforcement.

Understanding the Data

Across Australia, traffic offences are predominantly penalised through monetary fines rather than charges, or arrests. This data illustrates a consistent trend throughout the year, where each month sees hundreds of thousands of fines issued.

The steady distribution of fines across all states and months indicates that traffic enforcement is a routine part of legal administration, often automated through road safety cameras and patrol systems. These financial penalties form the primary mechanism for deterring offences like speeding, red light violations, and illegal parking.
